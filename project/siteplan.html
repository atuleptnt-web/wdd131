Tourist Sites in Ghana — Site Plan A living blueprint for design and development

Site name

Tourist Sites in Ghana

This name clearly communicates the website’s focus: showcasing Ghana’s most compelling destinations—historical, cultural, and natural—while helping visitors decide what best suits their interests.

Optional domain availability: touristsitesghana.com (not required for class)

Logo concept: Wordmark with Ghana gold heading color and a simple Kente-inspired accent stripe.

Site purpose

The site guides travelers through Ghana’s top tourist destinations with concise descriptions, images, and “What to Expect” highlights. It provides interactive filters (by region and type), travel tips, and references, helping users quickly find places aligned with their interests and plan memorable visits.

  • Content: Photos, names, descriptions, and expectations for each site
  • Tools: Interactive filters (Region, Type), saved preferences, accessible forms
  • Outcome: A user-friendly starting point for exploring Ghana’s history, culture, and nature

Scenarios

  • Family traveler: “Which sites near Cape Coast are kid-friendly and offer guided tours?”
  • Nature enthusiast: “Where can I see wildlife and waterfalls within a weekend itinerary?”
  • History student: “Which historical sites in Greater Accra and Central Regions provide curated exhibits?”

Color schema

Colors draw from Ghana’s visual identity. Gold emphasizes headings; forest green accents sections; deep blue improves link and button visibility; neutral text ensures readability.

Primary (Gold): #D4AF37 — Headings, highlights
Secondary (Green): #2E7D32 — Section borders, accents
Accent (Deep blue): #1565C0 — Links, buttons
Text (Neutral): #222222 — Body copy

Typography

Two complementary fonts ensure clear hierarchy and readability:

  • Headings: Merriweather (serif, bold) — used for h1–h3, highlights
  • Body: Inter (sans-serif) — used for paragraphs, lists, UI text

Heading sample — Merriweather

Body sample — Inter

Testing and validation plan

  • HTML/CSS validation: Validate pages using W3C validators; remove unused CSS rules.
  • Accessibility: Check keyboard navigation, focus states, alt text, and heading order (WCAG AA contrast).
  • Performance: Optimize images (WebP/JPEG), apply lazy loading, defer scripts.
  • Best practices & SEO: Use descriptive titles, meta descriptions, meaningful link text, and canonical URLs.
  • Responsiveness: Test mobile (portrait/landscape), tablet, and large desktop breakpoints.
